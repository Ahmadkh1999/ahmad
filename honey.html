Honeypot cyber technique

A Honey Pot is a cybersecurity tool or technique used to detect, deflect, or study attempts by hackers or unauthorized users to gain access to information systems. It involves creating a trap or simulated vulnerable system that appears to be part of a network but is isolated and monitored.

Honeypots serve multiple purposes in cybersecurity, including:

1- Detection and Monitoring:

They help in detecting and observing unauthorized activities and potential security threats within a network.

2- Understanding Attack Techniques:

By analyzing the interactions with the honeypot, security professionals can gain insights into attack techniques, tools, and strategies used by hackers, aiding in the development of better security measures and defenses.

3- Distraction and Diversion:

Honeypots divert attackers away from actual critical systems, buying time for security teams to strengthen defenses and mitigate potential risks.

4- Research and Analysis:

They provide valuable data for security research, enabling the study of evolving cyber threats and vulnerabilities.
